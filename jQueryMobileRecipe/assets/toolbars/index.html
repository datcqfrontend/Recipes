jQuery Mobile Toolbars

Toolbar elements are often placed inside headers and footers - for "easy-access" navigation. A header can contain one or two buttons, while the footer has no limit.

Header Bars

Home

Welcome To My Homepage

Search

The header is located at the top of the page and usually contain a page title/logo or one or two buttons (typically home, options or search). You can add buttons to the left and/or to the right side in the header. The code below, will add a "Home" button to the left and a "Search" button to the right of the header title text:

  <div data-role="header">
    <a href="#" class="ui-btn ui-btn-left ui-icon-home ui-btn-icon-left">Home</a>
    <h1>Welcome To My Homepage</h1>
    <a href="#" class="ui-btn ui-btn-right ui-icon-search ui-btn-icon-left">Search</a>
  </div>

Footer Bars

Add Me On Facebook Add Me On Twitter Add Me On Instagram

The footer is located at the bottom of the page. The footer is more flexible than the header - it is more functional and changeable throughout pages, and can therefore contain as many buttons as needed. The buttons in the footer are not centered by default. To fix this, simply use CSS "text-align:center".

You can also group buttons in the footer horizontally or vertically.

Positioning Headers and Footers

The header and the footer can be positioned in three ways:

  • Inline - Default. Headers and footers are inline with the page content
  • Fixed - Headers and footers will remain positioned at the top and bottom of the page
  • Fullscreen - Behaves like fixed; headers and footers will remain positioned at the top and bottom, but also over the page content. It is also slightly see-through
Use the data-position attribute to position your headers and footers.
The fullscreen position is ideal for photos, images and videos.
Tapping the screen will hide and show headers and footers for both fixed and fullscreen positions.
